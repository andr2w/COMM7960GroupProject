Motivation#

Short video platforms have boosted the spreading of fake news videos. Compared with tradtional social media platforms, fake news video propagating in short video platforms is more likely to be trusted by normal users [1] [2].

Defintion of Fake News Video#

Following previous studies [1] [3], we define a fake news video as a video post that conveys false, inaccurate, or misleading information. Note that a video post may include not only the video itself, see following Figure.

On text-dominated social platforms, e.g., Facebook or Twitter, there are text paragraphs attached to the video. On the video-dominated social platforms, e.g., Douyin or TikTok, there are title and text description attached to the video. Some studies [4] [5] include the audio and social context, e.g., publisher profile and user comments, in the videos.

Fake News Video vs. Fake Video#

Fake News Videos are closely related but different from fake videos. Recall the fake news video is the video with the intention of spreading false or misleading information. Fake video, on the other hand, is a video that has been manipulated or produced by Photoshop-like tools and/or generative AI techniques.

The difference logic between fake news video and fake video is that:

  • A fake video can be used to create a fake news video.
  • Fake videos are not fully equal to fake news videos, e.g., movie production.
  • A video that is not manipulated but accompanied by false or misleading information will be considered a fake news video.
